### Article 1: Parties
THIS LEASE dated this 7th day of April, 2026 BETWEEN: Figure Eight (the "Landlord") AND Skema BS (the "Tenant") AND Calabria Corp (the "Guarantor")[cite: 71, 72, 73, 75, 77, 78, 79, 81, 82, 84].

### Article 2: Definitions
"Additional Rent" means all amounts payable by the Tenant under this Lease except Base Rent[cite: 90]. "Building" means all buildings, improvements, equipment, fixtures, property and facilities from time to time located at 101 Avenue of the Americas, New York City[cite: 92]. "Common Areas and Facilities" mean those portions of the Building areas not designated to be leased to tenants of the Building, and those lands, areas, buildings, improvements, facilities, utilities, equipment and installations which serve or are for the useful benefit of the Building[cite: 94, 95, 97]. "Leasable Area" means with respect to any rentable premises, the area expressed in square feet of all floor space[cite: 98]. "Rent" means the total of Base Rent and Additional Rent[cite: 101].

### Article 3: Intent of Lease
It is the intent of this Lease that rent will be on a gross rent basis meaning the Tenant will pay the Base Rent and any Additional Rent and the Landlord will be responsible for all other service charges related to the Premises[cite: 104, 105].

### Article 4: Leased Premises
The Landlord agrees to rent to the Tenant the office space municipally described as 101 Avenue of the Americas, New York City (the "Premises") for only the permitted use of: office[cite: 112, 113]. No pets or animals are allowed to be kept in or about the Premises without the prior written permission of the Landlord[cite: 114]. The Tenant is entitled to the use of parking on or about the Premises[cite: 116].

### Article 5: Term
The term of the Lease commences at 12:00 noon on April 30, 2026 and ends at 12:00 noon on July 6, 2033 (the "Term")[cite: 124]. Should the Tenant remain in possession after expiration, a month-to-month tenancy will be created[cite: 125]. Upon 30 days' notice, the Landlord may terminate the tenancy if the Tenant has defaulted in payment[cite: 126]. Upon 60 days' notice, the Landlord may terminate the tenancy if the Tenant fails to perform covenants[cite: 127].

### Article 6: Rent
The Tenant will pay a base rent of $10,000.00, payable per month, for the Premises (the "Base Rent")[cite: 128]. The Tenant will pay the Base Rent on or before the first of each and every month[cite: 130].

### Article 7: Guarantees
The Guarantor guarantees to the Landlord that the Tenant will comply with the Tenant's obligations and agrees to compensate the Landlord in full on demand for all liability resulting from any failure by the Tenant[cite: 134]. 

### Article 8: Use and Occupation
The Tenant will continuously occupy and utilize the entire Premises in the active conduct of its business in a reputable manner[cite: 137]. The Tenant will carry on its business to comply with all statutes, bylaws, rules and regulations[cite: 138, 139].

### Article 9: Security Deposit
The Tenant will pay the Landlord a security deposit equal to the amount of $10,000.00[cite: 142]. The Tenant may not use the Security Deposit as payment for the Rent[cite: 146].

### Article 10: Quiet Enjoyment
The Landlord covenants that on paying the Rent and performing the covenants, the Tenant will peacefully and quietly have, hold, and enjoy the Premises[cite: 150, 151].

### Article 11: Additional Rights on Reentry
If the Landlord reenters the Premises or terminates this Lease, the Landlord may use reasonable force for gaining admittance, may expel the Tenant, and may relet the Premises[cite: 158, 161, 162, 166].

### Article 12: Utilities and Other Costs
The Landlord is responsible for the payment of electricity, natural gas, water, sewer, telephone, internet and cable[cite: 191].

### Article 13: Insurance
The personal property of the Tenant is not insured by the Landlord[cite: 194]. The Tenant is responsible for insuring any structural components of the Building incorporated into the Premises[cite: 199]. Both parties are responsible for liability insurance[cite: 201].

### Article 14: Governing Law
This Lease will be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York[cite: 213, 214].
